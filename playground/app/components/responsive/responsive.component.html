To change a layout at a screen size, add a second attribute next to class. It takes exactly the same class name you would have written, and applies it only at that size. Drag the Screen width slider on the stages below — they are real, resizable screens.
Write this Applies when the screen is
fs.lt-xs="…"599px wide or narrower
fs.lt-sm="…"1023px wide or narrower
fs.lt-md="…"1439px wide or narrower
fs.lt-lg="…"1919px wide or narrower
fs.gt-xs="…"600px wide or wider
fs.gt-sm="…"1024px wide or wider
fs.gt-md="…"1440px wide or wider
fs.gt-lg="…"1920px wide or wider

lt means at or below that size, gt means above it. So fs.lt-sm and fs.gt-sm between them cover every screen width, with no gap and no overlap.
Worth knowing: only the things you name in the responsive attribute change. Above, the row centres its children, and that centring carries over when it becomes a column — which is why the boxes sit in the middle rather than stretching. Name an alignment in the attribute too (fs.lt-sm="fs-column.gap-none.align-normal") if you want them to stretch.
